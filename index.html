Research and Media

The CogFx Studies

My dissertation, focusing on the impact of green buildings on cognitive function. The CogFx Studies have changed the value proposition behind green buildings by linking improved environmental conditions with worker productivity.

Aviation

Research on pilot flight performance and mental health. Pilots exposured to higher levels of CO2 had higher failure rates on advanced maneuvers in a flight simulator

MA SMART

A big data approach to understanding the factors that influence student absenteeism. Schools in Massachusetts with more surrounding greenness and lower ambient air pollution had lower chronic absenteeism rates.

Take Care

Interview with Lorraine Rapp and Linda Lowen of NPR's Take Care on the 9 foundations of a healthy building: the factors in the built environment that influence health and well-being

Green Offices

Article in Fast Company covering the CogFx Studies. The piece focuses on the second CogFx study, which found improved cognitive function among office workers in green certified buildings.

Bicyclists and Air Pollution

Article in the Boston Globe covering our paper on bicyclists' exposure to air pollution. Bike lanes that were separated from traffic or protected by vegetation has lower levels of air pollutants
